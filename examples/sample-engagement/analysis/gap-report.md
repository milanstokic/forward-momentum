## Blocking

### conflict-001 — conflict · requirement · **blocking**
- **Summary:** Account-model contradiction. The kickoff call commits to guest checkout — reach
  "order placed" with no account — while the product notes require every order to be tied to a
  signed-in Forkful account. Both cannot be true; this is the central decision the Resolution gate
  must force.
- **Severity rationale:** A direct contradiction at the heart of the flow. The entire screen shape,
  auth model, and the loyalty/chargeback rationale hang on which side wins. Blocking.
- **Related claims:** claim-004, claim-009
- **Evidence:**
  - `sources/kickoff-call.md` (L19): "Agreed, guest checkout is a must-have. If you have an item in
    your cart you should be able to get to \"order placed\" without ever creating an account."
  - `sources/kickoff-call.md` (L17): "Let people pay without signing up."
  - `sources/product-notes.md` (L15-L18): "Every order must be tied to a Forkful account"
  - `sources/product-notes.md` (L17-L18): "the checkout will require the user to be signed in before
    placing an order."

### gap-001 — gap · requirement · **blocking**
- **Summary:** Saved-card behavior is explicitly parked and never resolved, yet guest checkout and
  wallets ship in the same release. Whether/how saved cards work — and how they interact with guest
  checkout — is left undefined.
- **Severity rationale:** Payment-correctness requirement. "Parked" is not "resolved"; saved cards
  intersect both the account-model decision and the wallet work, so it must be settled before build.
  Blocking.
- **Related claims:** claim-008, claim-004, claim-006
- **Evidence:**
  - `sources/kickoff-call.md` (L31): "Let's — yeah, let's come back to the saved cards thing, I want
    to think about how that interacts with guest. Dani, can you note that as open."
  - `sources/product-notes.md` (L31): "Saved cards — how they behave, where they show. (Parked from
    kickoff.)"

### gap-002 — gap · design · **blocking**
- **Summary:** A declined / failed-payment error state is called out as a known pain point in the
  call, but no error-state frame exists in the design references — the designs stop at the happy
  path. Missing design on the payment step.
- **Severity rationale:** The pain point that the revamp explicitly exists to fix ("that has to feel
  less broken") has no mock. Payment can't ship correctly without the failure UX. Blocking; becomes
  a GitHub design task.
- **Related claims:** claim-010, claim-017, claim-018
- **Evidence:**
  - `sources/kickoff-call.md` (L35): "when a card gets declined right now, the user just sees the
    form again with no message. They retry the same card three times and rage-quit. Whatever we
    build, that has to feel less broken."
  - `sources/design-references.md` (L23): "Not yet mocked: still working through the back half of the
    flow. Will add frames as they're ready." (no error-state frame is listed in the frame table)

## Non-blocking

### gap-003 — gap · design · non-blocking
- **Summary:** The order-confirmation screen is described in the call as "the payoff" (order number,
  ETA, "we're on it") but no confirmation frame appears in the design references (the table stops at
  the place-order CTA) and it's absent from the product notes.
- **Severity rationale:** A real design gap, but the happy path up to "place order" can proceed while
  the confirmation screen is designed. Non-blocking; track as a design task.
- **Related claims:** claim-011, claim-017
- **Evidence:**

- **Related claims:** claim-012

### gap-005 — gap · requirement · non-blocking
